June 2014 DataDive

Want to use data for good? Read on



We’d like to invite you to take part in the next DataKind UK DataDive, on the weekend of 6th-8th June 2014, in central London.

What is a DataDive?

DataDives are weekend-long events, where a group of data and technology professionals work intensively with three pre-selected charities to help those charities use data better. The professional volunteers are people whose day jobs involve analysing and finding insights in data for multinational organisations. These specialists are not usually affordable to the charity and third sector: a DataDive is a way for charities to get these skills pro bono. The types of questions and challenges we look at might include:
  • Where and with what types of clients do our services have most impact? How might we target those clients better?
  • What national patterns of need do we see in our particular charitable area? Is there anything we’re missing which might help us have more effect?
  • Are there better ways to describe our work to funders and to measure what we do?
In the past we’ve worked on a wide variety of ideas, and with large and small organisations, including:
  • Oxfam GB
  • Keyfund
  • HelpAge International
  • Hampshire County Council SEN team
  • Community Voluntary Action Thameside
  • Place2Be

What is DataKind UK?

Organisations can gain huge advantages by using data in an intelligent and analytical way. However, managing and making sense of data needs skilled professionals, and those professionals are hard for charities to get access to.

At the same time, many of those professionals would love to use their skills to achieve something good. DataKind UK is a vehicle for that pro bono work, organising DataDives and longer term relationships to help charities benefit from the best of these skills and technologies. It was set up by a group of UK data professionals, to give local focus to the already very successful international DataKind charity.

DataKind UK is a charity run by volunteers and funded by donations. This DataDive is kindly sponsored by Nesta.

How do we get involved?

We are currently selecting the three charities for our June DataDive. If you are one of the selected charities, participation will involve: Meeting with assigned Data Ambassadors before the event, to define the problems and questions you’d like volunteers to tackle, make sure that the Ambassadors properly understand what you need, and gather and check the necessary data. Having at least one senior representative from your charity attend the weekend event in London from 6th to 8th June, to work alongside your assigned team of professionals, comment on their emerging findings, and guide their analysis with your knowledge of the subtleties of how your charity works. Meeting with your Data Ambassadors after the event, to get answers to any followup questions about the analysis, and discuss next steps. We expect that many charities will have follow-on questions and work which they may want to do: DataKind can provide a framework for longer-term pro bono support from just one or two professionals.
